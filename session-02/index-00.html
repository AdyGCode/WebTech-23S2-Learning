Hexadecimal and Decimal

Decimal counts in tens. It uses the numbers 0 to 9. When we get to 9, the next number is 10.

Hexadecimal counts in sixteens. It uses the numbers 0 to 9 and the letters A to F to represent 10 to 15.

So, the number A in "hex" is 10 in decimal.

So, the number 10 in "hex" is 16 in decimal (16 * 1 + 0).

Thus if we have FF in "hex", it is the same as 16*15 + 15, which is 240 + 15, or 255.

So, 8E would be 16*8 + 14, which is 128 + 14 = 142.

CC (Hex) is 16*12 + 12 = 192 + 12 = 204.
